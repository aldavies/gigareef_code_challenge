REMOTE TREASURE ISLAND

2000 kilometers off the coast of Africa might
not make Mauritius a first choice when it
comes to picking a tropical getaway, but its
remoteness is exactly what makes it well worth
visiting. The island's isolated spot in the Pacific ocean and its volcanic origin
contributed to it being a home to some of the rarest plants and animals
in the world, most famously the now extinct dodo bird. Its unique
position also made Mauritius susceptible to the cultures of nations that
have ruled over it. The Portuguese, Dutch, French and the British all had
a big role in influencing the way Mauritians live and the multicultural
heritage is still alive today.
